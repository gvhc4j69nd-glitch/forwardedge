Why we exist.

We started Forward Edge after years of watching public sector leaders pay for advice they couldn't act on. The dynamic is familiar: a recognizable firm wins the procurement, a junior team lands on-site, and three months later a deliverable arrives that mostly restates the briefing materials — with a vendor preference baked into the recommendation.

The cost is not the invoice. The cost is the year the agency spends recovering from the wrong recommendation, made by people who had never run the system they were redesigning.

Forward Edge is the alternative we wished existed. A small, senior, accountable team that brings practitioner experience to every conversation — and stays embedded long enough for the engagement to land.

We focus on the public sector because the stakes are concrete and the work is generational. The system you modernize today determines whether a Georgia veteran can renew benefits without taking a day off work, whether a state Medicaid program can defend its AI eligibility model in a hearing, whether a federal agency can stop the next breach before it hits the inspector general's report.

The work is hard. The work is worth it. That is enough.
